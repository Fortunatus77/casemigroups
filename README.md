The first program, SemigroupCA_deterministic_solutions.py, "experimentally" verifies correctness of 
deterministic solution formulae for any of the 18 ternary CA rules induced by semigroups. Just change the number in line "rule=number", the numbers 1..18 are
the same  as in the paper "Ternary cellular automata induced by semigroups of order 3 are solvable".

 The output consists of three pictures:
(1) pattern generated by the CA rule by normal iterations;
(2) the same pattern generated from the solution formula;
(3) the difference between them (should be empty - all white).

The second program, SemigroupCA_probabilistic_solutions.py,produces graphs of probabilities 
of occurrences of symbols 0,1,2 from experiment and theoretical formula for any of the 18 ternary CA rules induced by semigroups.
Just change the line "rule=number". You can also change initial probabilities p0,p1 as needed.

 The output consists of: 
(1) three contunuous curves, these are plots of theoretical formulae for  P(0),P(1), P(2) vs. n
2) three plots of data points marked with circles, these correspond the numerical values of P(0),P(1), P(2)
  obtained by iterating the CA rule on array of length M for T time steps.
Note: M must be large, if it is too small datapoints may not lay on theoretical curves - this is
because theoretical curves correspond to infinite M.
